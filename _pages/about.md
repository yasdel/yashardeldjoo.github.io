<h1 class="post-title">About me</h1>
</header>

<p> In my current position as a tenure-track assistant professor at the <a href="https://deipoliba.azurewebsites.net/en/department/" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">DEI</a>, Polytechnic University of Bari (Italy), I advise a team of PhD and MSc level students working on various aspects of personalized machine learning (PML) and recommender systems (RS). I have been a visiting researcher to the University of San Diego (USA). I earned my Ph.D. in computer science with a focus on recommender systems from Polytechnic University of Milan (Italy), where I also spent time as a visiting scholar at Johannes Kepler University Linz (Austria). At the moment, my research interest focouses on the <b>"consequences of personalization"</b> in PML systems.  Recently, I have developed a keen interest in the topic of <b>"causation and counterfactual interventions"</b>. I have contributed to a notable number of literature reviews, synthesizing the developments in the field of <b>"trustworthy Ml in recommender Systems"</b> and <b>"multimodal information-seeking"</b> on the widely-read ACM Computing Surveys (CSUR), and similar prestigous journals (<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.02757" target="\_blank" style="color:#B509AC">CSUR'23</a>, <a href="https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3439729" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">CSUR'22</a>, <a href="https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3407190" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">CSUR'21</a>, <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.11127" target="\_blank" style="color:">UMUAI'23</a>, <a href="https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3407190" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">CSUR'21</a>, <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2107.11803" target="\_blank" style="color:">IP&M'23</a>, <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2107.11803" target="\_blank" style="color:">CS-Review'23</a>. Below is a brief summary of my past and current research.<br/> <br />
  
  The recommender systems community at large acknowledges my work as pioneering contributions to the study of <b>"multi-modal information access" </b> focoused in particular on the development of various form of MM recommender systems and conversational agents. Important steps forward in the evolution of these systems have been taken due to my efforts, especially (<a href="https://doi.org/10.1145/3511808.3557656" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">CIKM'22</a>, <a href="https://doi.org/10.1145/3404835.3462806" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">SIGIR'21</a>üèÜ,  <a href="https://doi.org/10.1109/CVPRW53098.2021.00445" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">CVPR'21</a>,  <a href="https://doi.org/10.1007/s11257-019-09221-y" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">UMUAI'19</a>, <a href="https://doi.org/10.1145/3240323.3240407" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">RecSys'18</a>, <a href="https://doi.org/10.1145/3109859.3109908" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">RecSys'17</a>). Check out my most recent surveys on the above stated topics, which were written in collaboration with leading scholars and companies  such as Amazon, Spotify etc. (<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.02757" target="\_blank" style="color:#B509AC">CSUR'23</a>, <a href="https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-1-0716-2197-4_25" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">RSHB'22-MM</a>, <a href="https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3407190" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">CSUR'21</a>, <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2107.11803" target="\_blank" style="color:">CS-Review'23</a>, <a href="https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s13735-018-0154-2" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">IJMIR'18</a>). <br/> <br/>
  
  Lately, my research contributions have focused mostly on resolving issues associated with <b>"trustworthy ML"</b> for recommender systems in order to better comprehend their fairness and biases (<a href="https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ipm.2022.103115" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">IP&M'23</a>, <a href="https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3477495.3531959" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">FULL-SIGIR'22</a>, <a href="https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0306457321001503?via%3Dihub" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">IP&M'21</a>, <a href="https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11257-020-09285-1" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">UMUAI'21</a>), robustness under adversarial attacks (<a href="https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3511808.3557612" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">CIKM'22</a>, <a href="https://doi.org/10.1145/3404835.3462848" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">FULL-SIGIR'21</a>, <a href="https://doi.org/10.1145/3397271.3401046" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">Full-SIGIR'20</a>, <a href="https://doi.org/10.1145/3459637.3482046" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">CIKM'21üèÜ</a>, <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2107.13876" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">AdvML@KDD'21üèÜ</a>), privacy (<a href="https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-030-72113-8_3" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">ECIR'21</a> ), and interpretability (<a href="https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0306457321001503?via%3Dihub" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">IP&M'21</a>, <a href="https://doi.org/10.1016/j.eswa.2022.118368" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">ESWA'21</a>). Recent literature reviews that I have conducted on fairness (<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.11127" target="\_blank" style="color:">UMUAI'23</a>) and adversarial robustness in RS (<a href="https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3439729" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">CSUR'22</a>) have provided valuable contributions to synthesizing the developments in the field of trustworthy RS over the past several years and shedding light on future directions.
 <br/> <br />
  
I have recently had the privilege of contributing to two chapters (<a href="https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-1-0716-2197-4_25" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">RSHB'22-MM</a>, <a href="https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-1-0716-2197-4_9" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">RSHB'22-AdvML</a>) to the recommender systems handbook (3rd Edition, 2022), the <b>best book</b> currently in the RecSys community. From 2017 to 2020, I organized a series of machine-learning competitions in collaboration with significant industry companies including Twitter, Xing, and Spotify. I also hold a PATENT granted in 2019 (<a href="https://patentimages.storage.googleapis.com/7c/37/d4/c1c6a249bd3fa8/US10255503.pdf" target="\_blank" style="color: #B509AC">USPATENT'19)</a>.
